I am a senior undergraduate at Yale University pursuing a B.S. in astrophysics. My research interests broadly include Milky Way structure and dynamics, dwarf galaxies, and asteroseismology.

At Yale, I worked with Professor [Marla Geha](https://geha-group.github.io/) to constrain the chemical and dynamical properties of the ultra-faint dwarf galaxy Willman 1. I spent the 2024 summer as an REU student at the University of Hawai’i at Mānoa working with Professor [Dan Huber](https://home.ifa.hawaii.edu/users/dhuber/), Dr. [Yaguang Li](https://www.yaguangli.page/), and Dr. [Joel Ong](https://portfolio.hyad.es/) to constrain mass loss on the red giant branch using wide binaries and asteroseismology. This past summer, I studied Milky Way dynamics using Gaia DR3 at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) in Heidelberg, Germany with Professor [Hans-Walter Rix](https://www.mpia.de/rix) and [Vedant Chandra](https://vedantchandra.com/). Next year, I will be a visiting researcher at MPIA before beginning my Ph.D. in Astrophysics at Harvard in Fall 2027.

Outside of research, I enjoy [reading](https://www.goodreads.com/user/show/47228428-camille-chiu), listening to music, discovering places through photography, and biking.
