I am a visiting researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) in Heidelberg, Germany. My research interests broadly include Milky Way structure and dynamics, dwarf galaxies, and asteroseismology.

I received my B.S. in Astrophysics at Yale University in May 2026. At Yale, I worked with Professor [Marla Geha](https://geha-group.github.io/) to measure the chemical and dynamical properties of the ultra-faint dwarf galaxy Willman 1. I spent the 2024 summer as an REU student at the University of Hawai’i at Mānoa working with Professor [Dan Huber](https://home.ifa.hawaii.edu/users/dhuber/), Dr. [Yaguang Li](https://www.yaguangli.page/), and Dr. [Joel Ong](https://portfolio.hyad.es/) to constrain mass loss on the red giant branch using wide binaries and asteroseismology. Last summer, I studied Milky Way dynamics using Gaia DR3 at the MPIA with Professor [Hans-Walter Rix](https://www.mpia.de/rix) and [Vedant Chandra](https://vedantchandra.com/). Starting in the Fall 2027, I will begin my Ph.D. in Astronomy at Harvard University.

Outside of research, I enjoy [reading](https://www.goodreads.com/user/show/47228428-camille-chiu), [listening to music](https://open.spotify.com/user/mh711e310mxycu0b3816os052?si=7a05bd5bafe54170), discovering places through photography, & biking in bikeable cities. Also dancing & stargazing! My favorite star is the Sun.
