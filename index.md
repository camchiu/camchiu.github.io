I am a senior undergraduate at Yale University pursuing a B.S. in astrophysics. My research interests broadly include Milky Way structure and dynamics, dwarf galaxies, and asteroseismology.

At Yale, I worked with Marla Geha to constrain the chemical and dynamical properties of the ultra-faint dwarf galaxy Willman 1. I spent the summer 2025 as an REU student at the University of Hawai’i at Mānoa working with Professor Dan Huber, Dr. Yaguang Li, and Dr. Joel Ong to constrain mass loss on the red giant branch. This past summer, I studied Milky Way dynamics at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy in Heidelberg, Germany with Professor Hans-Walter Rix and Vedant Chandra.
